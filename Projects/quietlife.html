Quiet Life

Dungeon Dossier is currently in the design process and hasn't yet entered the development cycle. But that doesn't mean there isn't plenty to discuss. Dungeon Dossier arose after playing World of Warcraft for the first time. I was stunned by how tedious I found the game. The moment-to-moment gameplay to me was sluggish, almost entirely devoid of challenge, and if anything felt like it was disrespecting the time that I was putting into the game. Progress felt entirely artificial, and there was no way to meaningfully improve at the game, I don't think I played the game for more than a single week. After that initial experience I asked friends to tell me what they enjoyed about the game. The answers they gave were interesting and unexpected, they were more interested in optimizing their stats and load-outs than they were in the moment-to-moment gameplay. Additionally players found the relaxed pace, conducive to feeling like they were part of a world. Needless to say I disagreed with them but it did raise an interesting question. Can you create a game wherein players have no agency beyond equipping gear and distrubiting stat points, and will that game actually be fun.
